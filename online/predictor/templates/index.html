D-sORF


Small open reading frames (sORFs, <300nts) are widespread to all genomes. Since sORFs are much smaller than the majority of coding sequences (CDS) of protein genes they were often precluded in gene annotation. Even though sORFs may occur by chance part of them appear to be functional. Over the past few decades an increasing variety of peptides produced from sORF’s have been identified in various organisms, from bacteria to humans, as functional.

D-sORF is an algorithm that integrates statistical nucleotide context and motif information around the start codon for the identification of small coding ORFs.When D-sORF is applied on sORFs associated with ribosomes, the recognition of transcripts producing peptides (associated with Ensembl ID’s) is similar or superior with approaches based on Ribosome sequencing profiling. In parallel the recognition of putative negative data, for example ribosome associated transcripts form intron regions, remain remarkably low.
